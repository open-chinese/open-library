# Predominant

## Pronunciation
- IPA: /prɪˈdɒm.ɪ.nənt/
- Simplified: prih-DOM-ih-nuhnt

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Present as the strongest or main element; having the greatest influence, power, or importance.
2. Having or exerting control or authority; most frequent or common.

## Etymology
From Medieval Latin *praedominant-*, from *praedominare* ("to predominate"), from Latin *prae-* ("before, above") + *dominare* ("to rule, dominate"), from *dominus* ("lord, master"). Entered English in the mid-16th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "English has become the predominant language of international business and scientific research." -- *The Economist*
2. "The predominant view among climate scientists is that human activity is the primary driver of global warming." -- *Nature*
3. "Agriculture remains the predominant economic activity in many developing nations." -- *The New York Times*
4. "The predominant color in the artist's later work shifted from bright primary tones to muted earth tones."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** dominant, prevailing, primary, chief, principal, main, leading, paramount
**Antonyms:** minor, secondary, subordinate, insignificant, marginal, subsidiary

## Word Family
- **Verb:** predominate
- **Adverb:** predominantly
- **Noun:** predominance

## Collocations
- predominant view/opinion
- predominant role/factor
- predominant culture/language
- predominant feature/characteristic
- predominantly used/found

## TOEFL Tips
- The adverb "predominantly" is one of the most common adverbs in academic English and appears frequently on the TOEFL.
- Often used in reading passages to describe the main characteristic or leading factor in a process.
- Understanding this word helps answer main idea and detail questions about what is most important in a passage.
- Commonly appears in lectures about cultural, economic, and scientific topics.
