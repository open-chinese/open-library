# Formidable

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ňąf…Ēňźrm…™d…ôb…ôl/ or /f…Ēňźrňąm…™d…ôb…ôl/
- **Simplified:** FOR-mid-uh-buhl (preferred) or for-MID-uh-buhl

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Inspiring fear or respect through being impressively large, powerful, intense, or capable.
2. Difficult to deal with or overcome; presenting a serious challenge.

## Etymology
From Latin *formidabilis* ("causing fear, terrifying"), from *formidare* ("to fear, to dread"), from *formido* ("fear, dread, terror"). Entered English in the 15th century through French *formidable*. The word has gradually shifted from purely negative (terrifying) to a more mixed sense (impressively powerful or challenging).

## Usage Examples
1. "The research team faces the formidable task of analyzing over ten million data points collected during the five-year study." -- *Science*
2. "Despite her youth, she proved to be a formidable opponent in the debate, dismantling her rival's arguments with precision."
3. "Mount Everest remains a formidable challenge even for the most experienced mountaineers."
4. "The company has built a formidable reputation in the technology sector through continuous innovation."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** impressive, daunting, intimidating, imposing, fearsome, challenging, mighty, powerful, redoubtable
**Antonyms:** weak, insignificant, easy, feeble, unimpressive, trivial, negligible

## TOEFL Tips
- Appears in reading passages across many disciplines, often describing significant challenges or impressive achievements.
- In vocabulary questions, context determines whether the emphasis is on "impressive" or "difficult" -- both senses are commonly tested.
- Pronunciation note: both FOR-mid-uh-buhl and for-MID-uh-buhl are acceptable, but the first-syllable stress is more common in American English.
- Useful in TOEFL writing to describe significant challenges: "Climate change presents a formidable challenge to global food security."
