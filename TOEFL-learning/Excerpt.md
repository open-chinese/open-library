# Excerpt

## Definition
**Noun (countable)**
1. A short extract or passage taken from a longer piece of writing, music, or film.
2. A selected portion quoted or reproduced from a larger work.

**Verb (transitive)**
1. To take or select a passage or segment from a longer work.
2. To quote or reproduce a portion of a text, film, or musical composition.

## Etymology
From Latin *excerptum* ("something picked out"), neuter past participle of *excerpere* ("to pick out, select"), from *ex-* ("out") + *carpere* ("to pluck, gather"). Related to "carpet" (from the same root meaning "to pluck wool"). Entered English in the 16th century.

## TOEFL Tips
- Directly relevant to the TOEFL because the exam itself uses excerpts from academic texts and lectures. Understanding what an "excerpt" is helps you navigate the test format.
- In **reading sections**, instructions often mention that a passage is "excerpted from" a larger work. This means you are reading only a portion, and the full context may not be available.
- May appear in **vocabulary-in-context** questions where you need to identify "excerpt" as equivalent to "extract" or "passage."
- In the **writing section**, you may be asked to refer to or cite an excerpt from a reading passage when constructing your response.
