# Warrant

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˈwɒr.ənt/ (British), /ˈwɔːr.ənt/ (American)
- **Simplified:** WOR-unt

## Definition
**Verb**
1. To justify or necessitate a course of action; to make appropriate or reasonable
2. To officially affirm or guarantee; to give assurance

**Noun**
1. A document issued by a legal or government official authorizing action (such as a search or arrest)
2. Justification or authority for an action, belief, or feeling
3. (Finance) A document entitling the holder to receive goods, money, or shares

## Etymology
From Old North French *warant* ("protector, defender, authorization"), of Germanic origin, related to Old High German *werento* ("guarantor"). Entered English in the early 13th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The severity of the environmental damage warrants immediate government intervention." — *Nature*
2. "The judge issued a warrant for the seizure of financial records related to the investigation." — *The Washington Post*
3. "The preliminary findings warrant further investigation before any conclusions can be drawn." — *Journal of Clinical Investigation*
4. "The unprecedented scale of the humanitarian crisis warrants a coordinated international response." — *The Lancet*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms (verb):** justify, necessitate, call for, merit, authorize, sanction, validate
**Synonyms (noun):** authorization, permit, license, writ, order, justification
**Antonyms (verb):** invalidate, negate, undermine, discredit

## Word Family
- **Warranted** (adjective): justified or authorized
- **Unwarranted** (adjective): not justified or authorized; lacking good reason
- **Warranty** (noun): a written guarantee of product quality

## Collocations

## TOEFL Tips
"Warrant" is an important TOEFL word that functions as both a verb and a noun. The verb meaning ("to justify") is more commonly tested on the TOEFL than the legal noun meaning. In reading passages, "warrant" as a verb often appears in academic arguments where the author evaluates whether evidence is sufficient to support a conclusion ("the data warrant further study"). In vocabulary questions, "justify" is the most common correct synonym for the verb. The adjective "unwarranted" (meaning "unjustified") also appears frequently. In the TOEFL writing section, using "warrant" demonstrates sophisticated vocabulary and precise argumentation.
