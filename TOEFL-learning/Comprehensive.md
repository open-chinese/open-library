# Comprehensive

## Pronunciation
- IPA: /ˌkɒm.prɪˈhen.sɪv/
- Simplified: kom-prih-HEN-siv

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Complete; including all or nearly all elements or aspects of something.
2. Of large content or scope; wide-ranging.
3. (Of insurance) Providing coverage for most risks, including damage to the policyholder's own vehicle.

## Etymology
From French *comprehensif*, from Late Latin *comprehensivus*, from Latin *comprehensus*, past participle of *comprehendere* ("to grasp, comprise"), from *com-* ("together") + *prehendere* ("to seize"). Entered English in the early 17th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The report provides a comprehensive analysis of the economic factors contributing to income inequality." -- *The Economist*
2. "Congress passed comprehensive immigration reform after months of intense debate." -- *The New York Times*
3. "The university offers a comprehensive curriculum that covers both theoretical and applied sciences."
4. "A comprehensive review of the existing literature revealed significant gaps in our understanding of the disease." -- *The Lancet*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** thorough, complete, exhaustive, all-inclusive, extensive, sweeping, broad
**Antonyms:** incomplete, partial, limited, narrow, selective, superficial

## Word Family
- **Noun:** comprehensiveness, comprehension
- **Adverb:** comprehensively
- **Verb:** comprehend

## Collocations
- comprehensive review/analysis
- comprehensive plan/strategy
- comprehensive reform
- comprehensive study/survey
- comprehensive coverage/insurance

## TOEFL Tips
- Very common in academic reading passages, especially those describing research studies or policy proposals.
- Often appears in lecture contexts when professors describe thorough approaches to a subject.
- Useful in integrated writing tasks to describe a passage that covers multiple aspects of a topic.
- "Comprehensive" and "comprehension" (understanding) share a root but have distinct meanings -- be careful not to confuse them.
