# Lucrative

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˈluː.krə.tɪv/
- **Simplified:** LOO-kruh-tiv

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Producing a great deal of profit; financially rewarding
2. Yielding monetary gain; profitable

## Etymology
From Latin *lucrativus* ("gainful, profitable"), from *lucratus*, past participle of *lucrari* ("to gain, profit"), from *lucrum* ("profit, gain"). Entered English in the early 15th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The technology sector continues to offer some of the most lucrative career opportunities for recent graduates." — *Forbes*
2. "International trade in rare earth minerals has become an increasingly lucrative enterprise." — *The Economist*
3. "What began as a modest side project evolved into a lucrative business generating millions in annual revenue." — *Inc. Magazine*
4. "The pharmaceutical industry remains one of the most lucrative sectors in the global economy." — *The Financial Times*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** profitable, remunerative, gainful, rewarding, moneymaking, productive, worthwhile
**Antonyms:** unprofitable, worthless, unrewarding, loss-making, unproductive

## Word Family
- **Lucratively** (adverb): in a lucrative manner
- **Lucrativeness** (noun): the quality of being lucrative
- **Lucre** (noun): money or profit, especially when regarded as sordid or distasteful

## Collocations

## TOEFL Tips
"Lucrative" most commonly appears in TOEFL reading and listening passages about economics, business, and career topics. It always carries a positive connotation when describing financial outcomes. In vocabulary-in-context questions, "profitable" is the most straightforward synonym. The word is particularly useful in the TOEFL writing section when discussing economic topics or career choices. Note that "lucrative" specifically refers to monetary profit, not general satisfaction or personal fulfillment. It is a more formal and precise alternative to "profitable" that demonstrates strong vocabulary command.
