# Incentive

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ɪnˈsɛn.tɪv/
- **Simplified:** in-SEN-tiv

## Definition
**Noun**
1. A thing that motivates or encourages someone to do something; a reward or benefit offered to stimulate action
2. A payment or concession to stimulate greater output or investment

**Adjective**
1. Tending to motivate or encourage; serving as an incentive

## Etymology
From Late Latin *incentivum* ("that which sets the tune"), from *incentivus* ("setting the tune"), from Latin *incinere* ("to sing, strike up"), from *in-* ("in, upon") + *canere* ("to sing"). The meaning shifted from "inciting" to "motivating" over time. Entered English in the mid-15th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "Tax incentives for renewable energy investment have driven significant growth in the solar industry." — *Bloomberg New Energy Finance*
2. "The company introduced performance-based incentives to boost employee productivity and retention." — *Harvard Business Review*
3. "Without proper incentives, landowners have little motivation to adopt sustainable farming practices." — *Journal of Environmental Management*
4. "Financial incentives alone are insufficient to address the complex challenges of urban education reform." — *The Chronicle of Higher Education*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** motivation, stimulus, inducement, encouragement, spur, impetus, enticement, reward
**Antonyms:** deterrent, disincentive, discouragement, penalty, hindrance, obstacle

## Word Family
- **Incentivize** (verb): to provide someone with an incentive; to motivate with rewards
- **Incentivization** (noun): the process of providing incentives
- **Disincentive** (noun): a factor that discourages a particular action

## TOEFL Tips
"Incentive" appears frequently in TOEFL passages dealing with economics, public policy, business, and behavioral science. It is typically used to describe mechanisms that motivate behavior, whether through rewards (positive incentives) or penalties (negative incentives/disincentives). In reading passages, pay attention to how incentives are presented -- authors may argue for or against specific incentive structures. The word is also valuable for TOEFL writing, where you can discuss incentives as a way to support arguments about motivation, policy, or behavioral change. Common synonyms in TOEFL contexts include "motivation" and "stimulus."
