# Stipulate

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˈstɪp.jʊ.leɪt/
- **Simplified:** STIP-yoo-layt

## Definition
**Verb**
1. To demand or specify a requirement, typically as part of an agreement or contract
2. To state clearly and firmly as a condition or requirement
3. (Law) To make an agreement or covenant; to guarantee something in an agreement

## Etymology
From Latin *stipulatus*, past participle of *stipulari* ("to demand a formal promise, bargain"), possibly related to *stipula* ("stalk, straw"), from an ancient practice of breaking a straw to seal a bargain. Entered English in the early 17th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The contract stipulates that all work must be completed within six months of the signing date." — *Harvard Law Review*
2. "International trade agreements often stipulate specific environmental standards that member nations must meet." — *The Economist*
3. "The grant stipulates that the funds be used exclusively for research purposes." — *National Science Foundation*
4. "University regulations stipulate a minimum grade point average for students to maintain their scholarships." — *The Chronicle of Higher Education*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** specify, require, demand, state, set out, prescribe, dictate, mandate, lay down
**Antonyms:** imply, suggest, hint, leave open, waive

## Word Family
- **Stipulation** (noun): a condition or requirement specified in an agreement
- **Stipulated** (adjective): specified or agreed upon as a condition

## Collocations
- stipulate that
- stipulate a condition
- stipulate requirements
- clearly stipulate
- as stipulated in
- stipulate the terms

## TOEFL Tips
"Stipulate" frequently appears in TOEFL reading passages related to law, contracts, policy, and formal agreements. It is a formal verb that signals an explicit requirement or condition. In vocabulary questions, "specify" and "require" are the most common correct synonyms. Understanding this word is essential for passages about regulations, treaties, and institutional policies. In the TOEFL writing section, "stipulate" is useful for discussing rules and requirements in a formal, academic register. Pay attention to the common structure "stipulate that" followed by a clause describing the specific requirement.
