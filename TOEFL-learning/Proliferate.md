# Proliferate

## Pronunciation
- IPA: /prəˈlɪf.ə.reɪt/
- Simplified: pruh-LIF-uh-rayt

## Definition
**Verb**
1. To increase rapidly in number; to multiply.
2. To grow or reproduce by rapid production of new cells, parts, or offspring.
3. To cause something to spread or multiply.

## Etymology
Back-formation from *proliferation*, which comes from French *proliferation*, from *prolifere* ("producing offspring"), from Latin *proles* ("offspring") + *ferre* ("to bear, carry"). Entered English in the late 19th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "Social media platforms have proliferated over the past decade, fundamentally changing how people communicate." -- *The New York Times*
2. "Nuclear proliferation remains one of the most pressing security concerns of the international community." -- *The Economist*
3. "Invasive species can proliferate unchecked in ecosystems that lack natural predators." -- *National Geographic*
4. "Misinformation tends to proliferate rapidly online, outpacing efforts to fact-check and correct false claims."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** multiply, spread, increase, expand, mushroom, burgeon, escalate
**Antonyms:** decrease, diminish, dwindle, shrink, decline, contract

## Word Family
- **Noun:** proliferation
- **Adjective:** prolific (related but distinct)

## Collocations
- proliferate rapidly/quickly
- nuclear proliferation
- weapons proliferation
- continue to proliferate
- proliferate across/throughout

## TOEFL Tips
- The noun "proliferation" is extremely common in TOEFL academic passages, especially those about technology, biology, and politics.
- "Nuclear proliferation" is a set phrase that appears frequently in social science passages.
- In biology-related passages, "proliferate" often refers to cell growth or species expansion.
- Understanding this word helps with main idea questions about growth or expansion trends.
