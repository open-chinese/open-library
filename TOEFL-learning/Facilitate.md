# Facilitate

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /fəˈsɪl.ɪ.teɪt/
- **Simplified:** fuh-SIL-ih-tayt

## Definition
**Verb**
1. To make an action or process easier or more achievable
2. To help bring about a particular outcome or result
3. To lead or guide a group discussion or meeting

## Etymology
From French *faciliter*, from Italian *facilitare*, from Latin *facilis* ("easy"), from *facere* ("to make, do"). Entered English in the early 17th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The new software platform was designed to facilitate collaboration among remote team members." — *MIT Technology Review*
2. "International trade agreements are intended to facilitate the flow of goods and services across borders." — *The Economist*
3. "The mediator was brought in to facilitate negotiations between the two disputing parties." — *Harvard Negotiation Law Review*
4. "Warm ocean temperatures facilitate the rapid intensification of tropical storms." — *Journal of Climate*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** enable, ease, assist, promote, expedite, aid, help, advance, simplify
**Antonyms:** hinder, impede, obstruct, hamper, inhibit, complicate, prevent

## Word Family
- **Facilitation** (noun): the process of facilitating
- **Facilitator** (noun): a person who facilitates; one who helps a group work together effectively
- **Facility** (noun): a place or equipment for doing something; ease in doing something
- **Facile** (adjective): appearing neat and comprehensive but lacking depth; easily achieved

## Collocations

## TOEFL Tips
"Facilitate" is one of the most commonly tested verbs on the TOEFL, appearing in reading and listening passages across all academic disciplines. It is a formal synonym for "make easier" and is widely used in academic writing. In vocabulary questions, "make easier" or "help to bring about" are standard correct answers. The word is particularly useful in the TOEFL writing section as a sophisticated alternative to simpler verbs like "help" or "assist." Note that "facilitate" implies removing obstacles or creating conditions for success, not doing the work itself.
