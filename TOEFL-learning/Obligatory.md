# Obligatory

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /əˈblɪɡ.ə.tɔːr.i/
- **Simplified:** uh-BLIG-uh-tor-ee

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Required by a legal, moral, or other rule; compulsory
2. (Of a ruling, law, or requirement) Having binding force; mandatory
3. (Biology) Restricted to a particular function or mode of life (e.g., obligatory parasite)

## Etymology
From Late Latin *obligatorius* ("binding"), from Latin *obligare* ("to bind, oblige"), from *ob-* ("to, toward") + *ligare* ("to bind, tie"). Entered English in the late 14th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "Attendance at the safety training session is obligatory for all new employees." — *Occupational Health & Safety*
2. "In many European countries, military service was once obligatory for all male citizens." — *Foreign Affairs*
3. "The curriculum includes an obligatory research component that all doctoral candidates must complete." — *Journal of Graduate Education*
4. "Certain species of fungi are obligatory parasites, meaning they can only survive on a living host." — *Mycological Research*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** mandatory, compulsory, required, requisite, binding, necessary, imperative
**Antonyms:** optional, voluntary, elective, discretionary, unnecessary, noncompulsory

## Word Family
- **Obligation** (noun): a duty or commitment; something one is bound to do
- **Oblige** (verb): to compel or require; to do a favor for
- **Obliged** (adjective): bound by duty or gratitude
- **Obligatorily** (adverb): in an obligatory manner

## Collocations
- obligatory requirement
- obligatory course
- obligatory attendance
- obligatory service
- make obligatory
- obligatory for all

## TOEFL Tips
"Obligatory" appears in TOEFL passages related to law, education, social customs, and biology. It signals that something is not optional but required. In vocabulary questions, "compulsory" and "mandatory" are the most common correct synonyms. Watch for the biological usage in science passages, where "obligatory" describes organisms that must live in a specific way (e.g., obligatory anaerobes that cannot survive in oxygen). In the TOEFL writing section, "obligatory" is an effective word for discussing rules, requirements, or duties, offering a more academic alternative to "required."
