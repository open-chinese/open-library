# Tangible

## Pronunciation
- IPA: /ˈtæn.dʒɪ.bəl/
- Simplified: TAN-jih-buhl

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Perceptible by touch; having physical substance.
2. Clear and definite; real and concrete rather than imaginary or abstract.
3. (Finance/Law) Of assets: having actual physical existence, such as real estate or equipment.

**Noun**
4. (Usually plural) A thing that is perceptible by touch; tangible assets.

## Etymology
From Late Latin *tangibilis* ("that may be touched"), from Latin *tangere* ("to touch"). Related to English words "tangent," "tactile," and "contact." Entered English in the late 16th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The new policy has yet to produce tangible results, despite the government's optimistic projections." -- *The Economist*
2. "Investors increasingly prefer tangible assets like real estate and gold during periods of economic uncertainty." -- *Financial Times*
3. "The benefits of the educational program are tangible: higher graduation rates and improved test scores." -- *The New York Times*
4. "While the emotional toll of the disaster was immeasurable, the tangible damage was estimated at billions of dollars."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** concrete, palpable, physical, real, material, substantial, touchable, evident
**Antonyms:** intangible, abstract, immaterial, imperceptible, theoretical, vague

## Word Family
- **Noun:** tangibility
- **Adverb:** tangibly
- **Antonym:** intangible

## Collocations
- tangible results/benefits
- tangible evidence/proof
- tangible assets/property
- tangible difference/impact
- tangible and intangible

## TOEFL Tips
- Often contrasted with "intangible" in TOEFL passages, especially in business and economics readings.
- Common in passages about measuring outcomes, evaluating policies, or distinguishing between physical and abstract qualities.
- Understanding the tangible/intangible distinction is valuable for inference and comparison questions.
- In the writing section, referring to "tangible evidence" or "tangible results" strengthens arguments.
