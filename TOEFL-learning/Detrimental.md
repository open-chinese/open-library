# Detrimental

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˌdɛt.rɪˈmɛn.təl/
- **Simplified:** det-rih-MEN-tul

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Tending to cause harm; damaging or injurious
2. Having a negative or harmful effect on something

## Etymology
From Latin *detrimentum* ("a rubbing off, loss, damage"), from *deterere* ("to wear away"), from *de-* ("away") + *terere* ("to rub, wear"). The adjective form with the suffix *-al* entered English in the mid-17th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "Prolonged exposure to excessive noise levels can be detrimental to hearing and overall health." — *World Health Organization*
2. "The study found that sleep deprivation is detrimental to cognitive performance and decision-making." — *Journal of Sleep Research*
3. "Invasive species can be detrimental to native ecosystems by competing for resources and altering habitats." — *Ecology Letters*
4. "The policy was criticized as detrimental to small businesses that lacked the resources to comply with new regulations." — *The Economist*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** harmful, damaging, injurious, adverse, deleterious, destructive, unfavorable, negative
**Antonyms:** beneficial, advantageous, helpful, constructive, positive, favorable

## Word Family
- **Detriment** (noun): the state of being harmed or damaged; a cause of harm or damage
- **Detrimentally** (adverb): in a detrimental manner

## Collocations
- detrimental effect
- detrimental to health
- detrimental impact
- prove detrimental
- potentially detrimental
- detrimental consequences
- environmentally detrimental

## TOEFL Tips
"Detrimental" is a high-frequency TOEFL vocabulary word that appears in reading passages about health, environment, economics, and social policy. It is almost always followed by "to" (detrimental to something). In vocabulary-in-context questions, "harmful" and "damaging" are the most common correct synonyms. The word carries a formal, academic tone, making it suitable for use in TOEFL writing tasks. It is often paired with its antonym "beneficial" in passages that compare positive and negative effects. Understanding this word helps with both reading comprehension and the ability to express negative impacts in writing.
