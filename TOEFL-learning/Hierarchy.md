# Hierarchy

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˈhaɪ.ə.rɑːr.ki/ (American) or /ˈhaɪ.ə.rɑː.ki/ (British)
- **Simplified:** HY-uh-rar-kee

## Definition
**Noun (countable and uncountable)**
1. A system or organization in which people or groups are ranked one above the other according to status, authority, or power.
2. An arrangement of items (objects, names, values) in which the items are represented as being above, below, or at the same level as one another.
3. The upper echelons of an organization; the people in authority. *The church hierarchy.*
4. (Biology, taxonomy) A classification system organized into successively subordinate groups.

## Etymology
From Old French *ierarchie*, from Medieval Latin *hierarchia*, from Greek *hierarkhia* ("rule of a high priest"), from *hierarkhes* ("leader of sacred rites"), from *hieros* ("sacred") + *arkhein* ("to lead, rule"). Originally referred to the ranking of angels; the secular sense emerged in the 17th century.

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** ranking, order, pecking order, stratification, grading, ladder, chain of command, class system
**Antonyms:** equality, egalitarianism, flatness, parity, uniformity

## TOEFL Tips
- Appears very frequently in **reading passages** about sociology, organizational behavior, biology (taxonomic classification), and history (social structures).
- Maslow's "hierarchy of needs" is a commonly referenced concept in TOEFL psychology passages.
- In biology passages, understanding "hierarchy" helps with questions about taxonomic classification (kingdom, phylum, class, order, etc.).
- The adjective "hierarchical" is equally important for the TOEFL and often describes organizational or social structures.
- Spelling can be tricky — note the "-ie-" after the "h" and the "-archy" ending.
