# Phenomenon

## Pronunciation
- IPA: /f…™Ààn…ím.…™.n…ôn/
- Simplified: fih-NOM-ih-nun
- Plural: phenomena (IPA: /f…™Ààn…ím.…™.n…ô/)

## Definition
**Noun**
1. A fact or situation that is observed to exist or happen, especially one whose cause or explanation is in question.
2. A remarkable person, thing, or event.
3. (Philosophy) An object of a person's perception; what the senses or mind notices, as opposed to the thing in itself (noumenon).

## Etymology
From Late Latin *phaenomenon*, from Greek *phainomenon* ("that which appears"), neuter present participle of *phainesthai* ("to appear"), from *phainein* ("to show"). Entered English in the late 16th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The phenomenon of urban heat islands has become a major area of study for climate researchers." -- *Nature*
2. "Social media addiction is a relatively new phenomenon that psychologists are only beginning to understand." -- *The New York Times*
3. "El Nino is a climate phenomenon that affects weather patterns across the globe." -- *National Geographic*
4. "The cultural phenomenon of remote work has permanently altered expectations about the modern workplace." -- *The Economist*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** occurrence, event, happening, fact, circumstance, experience, spectacle
**Antonyms:** (no direct antonyms; in philosophy) noumenon (the thing in itself)

## Word Family
- **Noun:** phenomenon (singular), phenomena (plural)
- **Adjective:** phenomenal
- **Adverb:** phenomenally

## Collocations
- natural/cultural/social phenomenon
- observe/study a phenomenon
- explain/understand a phenomenon
- widespread/common phenomenon
- phenomenon known as

## TOEFL Tips
- One of the most common nouns in TOEFL academic passages across all disciplines.
- The plural form "phenomena" is frequently used -- remember it is already plural (do not say "phenomenas").
- Often introduces the main topic of a reading passage or lecture.
- "Phenomenal" (adjective) means "remarkable" in everyday usage but "relating to phenomena" in philosophical contexts.
