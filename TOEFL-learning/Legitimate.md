# Legitimate

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət/ (adjective), /lɪˈdʒɪtɪmeɪt/ (verb)
- **Simplified:** lih-JIT-ih-mut (adjective), lih-JIT-ih-mayt (verb)

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Conforming to the law or to rules; lawful and authorized.
2. Able to be defended with logic or justification; reasonable and valid.
3. (Of a child) Born to parents who are legally married to each other.
4. (Of a sovereign) Having a title based on strict hereditary right.

**Verb (transitive)**
1. To make legitimate; to justify or validate.

## Etymology
From Medieval Latin *legitimatus*, past participle of *legitimare* ("to make lawful"), from Latin *legitimus* ("lawful, according to law"), from *lex/legis* ("law"). Entered English in the 15th century. The same root *lex* gives us "legal," "legislate," "legislature," and "privilege."

## Usage Examples
1. "The court ruled that the company had a legitimate claim to the intellectual property in question." -- *Harvard Law Review*
2. "Citizens have a legitimate right to question and challenge government policies through peaceful means."
3. "The humanitarian organization sought to address the legitimate concerns of refugees fleeing the conflict zone."
4. "Scholars continue to debate whether the election results can be considered legitimate given the reported irregularities."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** lawful, legal, valid, justified, rightful, authorized, genuine, proper, warranted
**Antonyms:** illegitimate, illegal, unlawful, invalid, unjustified, fraudulent, spurious

## TOEFL Tips
- Common in reading passages about law, politics, ethics, and social science.
- The noun "legitimacy" is equally important for TOEFL, especially in political science contexts (e.g., "the legitimacy of the government").
- Pay attention to the two pronunciations: the adjective ends in /-mut/ while the verb ends in /-mayt/.
- Often appears in TOEFL passages debating whether certain practices, claims, or authorities are valid.
- In TOEFL writing, "legitimate" is a strong academic word for expressing that something is valid: "This is a legitimate concern that deserves attention."
