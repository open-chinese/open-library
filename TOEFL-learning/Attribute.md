# Attribute

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˈæt.rɪ.bjuːt/ (noun), /əˈtrɪb.juːt/ (verb)
- **Simplified:** AT-rih-byoot (noun), uh-TRIB-yoot (verb)

## Definition
**Noun**
1. A quality or feature regarded as a characteristic or inherent part of someone or something
2. A quality ascribed to a person, thing, or group

**Verb**
1. To regard something as being caused by; to ascribe to a particular cause or source
2. To credit a work or remark to a particular author or source

## Etymology
From Latin *attributum*, past participle of *attribuere* ("to assign, allot, bestow"), from *ad-* ("to") + *tribuere* ("to assign, give"). Entered English in the 14th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "Researchers attribute the decline in bee populations to a combination of pesticide use and habitat loss." — *Nature*
2. "Resilience is often cited as a key attribute of successful entrepreneurs." — *Forbes*
3. "The painting was originally attributed to Rembrandt, but later analysis revealed it was the work of a student." — *The Art Bulletin*
4. "Scientists attribute the unusual weather patterns to shifting ocean currents influenced by climate change." — *Science*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms (noun):** quality, characteristic, feature, trait, property, aspect
**Synonyms (verb):** ascribe, credit, assign, impute, accredit
**Antonyms (verb):** dissociate, disconnect, separate

## Word Family
- **Attribution** (noun): the act of attributing; the ascribing of a cause or source
- **Attributable** (adjective): able to be attributed to a particular cause
- **Attributive** (adjective): relating to or denoting an attribute

## Collocations

## TOEFL Tips
"Attribute" is an extremely important TOEFL word because it functions as both a noun and a verb with different pronunciations and slightly different meanings. As a verb (stress on the second syllable), it means "to credit to a cause" and is common in science and social science passages. As a noun (stress on the first syllable), it means "a quality or characteristic." In vocabulary questions, the verb form is often tested with "ascribe" as the correct synonym. Pay careful attention to stress placement, as this distinction may appear in the listening section.
