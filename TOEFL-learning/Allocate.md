# Allocate

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˈæl.ə.keɪt/
- **Simplified:** AL-uh-kayt

## Definition
**Verb (transitive)**
1. To distribute or apportion (resources, funds, time, etc.) for a specific purpose.
2. To designate or set aside for a particular use or recipient.
3. To assign or earmark something according to a plan or system.

## Etymology
From Medieval Latin *allocatus*, past participle of *allocare* ("to place, assign"), from Latin *ad-* ("to") + *locare* ("to place"), from *locus* ("place"). First recorded in English in the early 17th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The federal government will **allocate** $2 billion to infrastructure improvements over the next five years." — *The New York Times*
2. "Universities must carefully **allocate** their limited research funding to maximize scientific output." — *Nature*
3. "The project manager decided to **allocate** additional staff to the quality assurance team." — *Harvard Business Review*
4. "Each department is required to **allocate** a portion of its budget to employee training and development."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** distribute, apportion, assign, designate, allot, earmark, dedicate, appropriate
**Antonyms:** withhold, retain, keep, hoard, collect, reclaim

## TOEFL Tips
- Frequently appears in **reading passages** about economics, government policy, and resource management.
- In **listening sections**, often used in lectures about budgeting, environmental resource distribution, or organizational planning.
- On the TOEFL, "allocate" typically implies a deliberate, planned distribution rather than a random one. Pay attention to context clues about who is distributing what and why.
- Common in academic discussions about how societies, governments, or institutions decide to use limited resources.
