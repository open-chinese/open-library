# Proximity

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /prɒkˈsɪm.ɪ.ti/ (British) or /prɑːkˈsɪm.ɪ.t̬i/ (American)
- **Simplified:** prok-SIM-ih-tee

## Definition
**Noun (uncountable or countable)**
1. Nearness in space, time, or relationship; the state of being close to something or someone.
2. The region or area close to a particular place. *In the proximity of the airport.*
3. Closeness in terms of kinship, connection, or resemblance.

## Etymology
From Latin *proximitas* ("nearness"), from *proximus* ("nearest, next"), superlative of *prope* ("near"). Related to "approximate," "proximate," and "approached." Entered English in the late 15th century through French *proximite*.

## Usage Examples
1. "The **proximity** of the university to several major research hospitals facilitates collaboration between faculty and clinicians." — *The New England Journal of Medicine*
2. "Property values tend to increase in **proximity** to public transportation networks." — *The Economist*
3. "The **proximity** of the two species' habitats has led to increased competition for food resources." — *Ecology*
4. "The castle's strategic location, in close **proximity** to the river, made it an ideal defensive position." — *Smithsonian Magazine*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** nearness, closeness, vicinity, adjacency, propinquity, neighborhood
**Antonyms:** distance, remoteness, farness, separation

## Collocations
- **in proximity to** / **in close proximity to**
- **geographical** / **physical** / **spatial** proximity
- proximity **to** a location / resource / market
- **close** / **immediate** proximity
- the **proximity** of X to Y

## TOEFL Tips
- Appears often in **reading passages** about geography, urban planning, ecology, and history where the spatial relationship between places or organisms matters.
- The phrase "in close proximity to" is common in academic English. Note that some style guides consider "close proximity" redundant (since "proximity" already implies closeness), but it frequently appears in TOEFL passages.
- In **listening sections**, professors may discuss how the "proximity" of certain factors influences outcomes (e.g., proximity to water sources affecting settlement patterns).
- Understanding "proximity" helps with questions about cause-and-effect relationships, since spatial closeness is often presented as a factor influencing outcomes in academic texts.
- The related word "approximate" (meaning "close to" or "nearly exact") also appears frequently on the TOEFL.
