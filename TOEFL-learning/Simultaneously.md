# Simultaneously

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˌsaɪ.məlˈteɪ.ni.əs.li/
- **Simplified:** sy-mul-TAY-nee-us-lee

## Definition
**Adverb**
1. At the same time; occurring, operating, or done at the same time
2. In a way that involves two or more things happening or being done at once

## Etymology
From Medieval Latin *simultaneus*, from Latin *simul* ("at the same time"), related to *similis* ("like, similar"). The adverb form adds the suffix *-ly* to *simultaneous*. Entered English in the 17th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The satellite can simultaneously transmit data to multiple ground stations across different continents." — *IEEE Communications Magazine*
2. "The experiment required participants to simultaneously process visual and auditory stimuli." — *Journal of Cognitive Neuroscience*
3. "Several countries simultaneously announced new trade agreements at the international summit." — *The New York Times*
4. "The artist simultaneously explored themes of identity and displacement in her latest exhibition." — *The Guardian*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** concurrently, at the same time, in unison, at once, in tandem, synchronously, coincidentally
**Antonyms:** sequentially, separately, consecutively, successively, one after another

## Word Family
- **Simultaneous** (adjective): occurring or done at the same time
- **Simultaneity** (noun): the quality or state of being simultaneous
- **Simultaneousness** (noun): the condition of occurring at the same time

## TOEFL Tips
"Simultaneously" is a high-frequency word in TOEFL listening and reading sections, particularly in science and history passages. It often appears when describing parallel processes, concurrent events, or multitasking. In listening passages, pay attention to the speaker's use of this word to understand the temporal relationship between events. In the integrated writing task, it can be useful for describing events that happen at the same time. Remember that it always implies exact temporal overlap, not merely close proximity in time.
