# Pervasive

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /pərˈveɪ.sɪv/
- **Simplified:** pur-VAY-siv

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Spreading widely throughout an area or group of people; existing in or spreading through every part of something
2. Having the quality of pervading or permeating; present and noticeable in every part

## Etymology
From Latin *pervadere* ("to go through, spread through"), from *per-* ("through") + *vadere* ("to go"), combined with the suffix *-ive*. First recorded in English in the mid-18th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The pervasive influence of social media on modern communication has fundamentally changed how people interact." — *The Atlantic*
2. "Air pollution remains a pervasive problem in many rapidly industrializing nations." — *World Health Organization Report*
3. "There is a pervasive sense of optimism among researchers working on renewable energy technologies." — *Nature*
4. "The pervasive use of smartphones has transformed consumer behavior across all demographics." — *Harvard Business Review*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** widespread, prevalent, ubiquitous, omnipresent, extensive, rampant, universal
**Antonyms:** limited, restricted, confined, rare, uncommon, localized

## Word Family
- **Pervade** (verb): to spread through and be perceived in every part of
- **Pervading** (adjective): spreading throughout
- **Pervasively** (adverb): in a pervasive manner
- **Pervasiveness** (noun): the quality or state of being pervasive

## Collocations

## TOEFL Tips
"Pervasive" frequently appears in TOEFL reading passages related to social phenomena, technology, and environmental issues. It is used to describe something that is found everywhere or has a wide-reaching effect. In listening sections, it may appear in academic lectures discussing cultural trends or ecological changes. Be prepared to distinguish it from "prevalent," which emphasizes frequency, whereas "pervasive" emphasizes thorough penetration into all areas.
