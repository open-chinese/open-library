# Ubiquitous

## Pronunciation
- IPA: /juːˈbɪk.wɪ.təs/
- Simplified: yoo-BIK-wi-tuhs

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Present, appearing, or found everywhere; seeming to be in all places at the same time.
2. Constantly encountered; widespread.

## Etymology
From Latin *ubique* ("everywhere"), derived from *ubi* ("where") + *-que* ("and, also"). First used in English in the early 17th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "Smartphones have become so ubiquitous that it is now unusual to see someone without one." -- *The Economist*
2. "The ubiquitous coffee chain has opened yet another location in the downtown area." -- *The New York Times*
3. "Plastic pollution has become ubiquitous in the world's oceans, reaching even the most remote marine environments." -- *Nature*
4. "Wi-Fi has become ubiquitous in urban areas, transforming how people work and communicate."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** omnipresent, pervasive, universal, widespread, prevalent, everywhere
**Antonyms:** rare, scarce, uncommon, limited, localized

## Word Family
- **Noun:** ubiquity, ubiquitousness
- **Adverb:** ubiquitously

## Collocations
- ubiquitous presence
- ubiquitous technology
- become ubiquitous
- nearly/almost ubiquitous
- ubiquitous in modern life

## TOEFL Tips
- Frequently appears in reading passages about technology, globalization, and cultural trends.
- Often used in academic lectures describing widespread phenomena.
- In listening sections, pay attention to context clues like "everywhere" or "all around us" that signal this word's meaning.
- A strong vocabulary word for the writing section when discussing trends or widespread issues.
