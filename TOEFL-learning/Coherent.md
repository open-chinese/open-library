# Coherent

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /koʊˈhɪərənt/
- **Simplified:** koh-HEER-uhnt

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Logical and consistent; forming a unified, intelligible whole.
2. (Of a person) able to speak clearly and logically.
3. (Physics) Of waves: having a constant phase relationship, as in laser light.

## Etymology
From Latin *cohaerens*, present participle of *cohaerere* ("to stick together, to be connected"), from *co-* ("together") + *haerere* ("to stick, to cling"). The word entered English in the 16th century, originally meaning "physically sticking together" before developing the figurative sense of "logically connected."

## Usage Examples
1. "The research team presented a coherent argument supported by extensive empirical data." -- *Nature*
2. "After the accident, the witness was too shaken to give a coherent account of what had happened."
3. "The government needs a coherent policy on climate change rather than a patchwork of contradictory measures."
4. "Her essay was well-organized and coherent, moving smoothly from one point to the next."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** logical, consistent, rational, orderly, systematic, lucid, articulate, intelligible
**Antonyms:** incoherent, disjointed, illogical, muddled, confused, rambling, inconsistent

## TOEFL Tips
- Extremely common in TOEFL writing and speaking scoring rubrics -- your own responses are expected to be "coherent."
- Appears in reading passages about argumentation, scientific theories, and organizational structures.
- Often tested alongside "cohesive" (which emphasizes unity through linking devices, while "coherent" emphasizes logical consistency).
- In integrated tasks, you may need to describe whether a lecture is coherent with a reading passage.
