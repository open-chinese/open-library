# Subsequent

## Pronunciation
- IPA: /ˈsʌb.sɪ.kwənt/
- Simplified: SUB-sih-kwuhnt

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Coming after something in time; following.
2. Occurring or existing later or after something else.

## Etymology
From Latin *subsequens*, present participle of *subsequi* ("to follow closely"), from *sub-* ("under, after") + *sequi* ("to follow"). Entered English in the mid-15th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The initial outbreak and subsequent spread of the virus overwhelmed the region's healthcare infrastructure." -- *The New York Times*
2. "Subsequent research confirmed the findings of the original study, lending greater credibility to its conclusions." -- *Nature*
3. "The company's decision to expand into Asia and its subsequent success there transformed it into a global brand." -- *Financial Times*
4. "In subsequent chapters, the author explores the political implications of these economic changes."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** following, ensuing, succeeding, later, successive, next
**Antonyms:** previous, prior, preceding, former, earlier, antecedent

## Word Family
- **Adverb:** subsequently
- **Noun:** subsequence (rare)

## Collocations
- subsequent to (something)
- subsequent analysis/research
- subsequent events/developments
- subsequent years/decades
- in subsequent chapters/sections

## TOEFL Tips
- Extremely common in academic reading and listening passages to sequence events or findings.
- "Subsequently" is a key transition word for both understanding lectures and writing essays.
- Often appears in history and science passages to describe cause-and-effect relationships.
- Pay attention to "subsequent to" as a formal way of saying "after" in TOEFL reading passages.
