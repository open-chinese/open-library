# Sporadic

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /spəˈræd.ɪk/
- **Simplified:** spuh-RAD-ik

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Occurring at irregular intervals or only in a few places; scattered or isolated.
2. Appearing or happening infrequently and without a regular pattern.
3. (Medicine) Of a disease occurring occasionally in isolated instances, not as an epidemic.

## Etymology
From Medieval Latin *sporadicus*, from Greek *sporadikos* ("scattered"), from *sporas* (genitive *sporados*) ("scattered, dispersed"), from *speirein* ("to sow, scatter"). Related to "spore" and "diaspora." Entered English in the late 17th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "**Sporadic** outbreaks of the disease continued to occur in rural areas despite the nationwide vaccination campaign." — *World Health Organization*
2. "The region experiences **sporadic** rainfall, making agriculture extremely unpredictable and challenging." — *National Geographic*
3. "**Sporadic** protests erupted across the country in response to the proposed legislation." — *BBC*
4. "Although fossil evidence of the species is **sporadic**, enough specimens have been found to establish its approximate range and diet."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** intermittent, irregular, occasional, infrequent, scattered, isolated, erratic, random, periodic
**Antonyms:** constant, continuous, regular, steady, persistent, frequent, systematic, consistent

## TOEFL Tips
- Appears in **reading passages** about epidemiology, climate, ecology, and history where irregular or infrequent events are described.
- On the TOEFL, "sporadic" is often contrasted with words like "constant," "consistent," or "systematic." Recognizing this contrast helps with vocabulary-in-context questions.
- In **listening sections**, professors may describe phenomena that happen "sporadically" to distinguish them from regular or predictable patterns.
- The adverb "sporadically" is equally common and means "from time to time, without regularity."
- Be careful not to interpret "sporadic" as meaning rare in absolute terms -- it emphasizes irregularity of timing and distribution rather than overall scarcity alone.
