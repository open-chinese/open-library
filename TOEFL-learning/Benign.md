# Benign

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /bɪˈnaɪn/
- **Simplified:** bih-NINE

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. (Medicine) Not harmful or malignant; not cancerous. *A benign tumor.*
2. Gentle, kindly, and favorable in nature or effect.
3. (Of climate or environment) Mild and favorable; not threatening.
4. Showing kindness and gentleness; well-meaning.

## Etymology
From Old French *benigne*, from Latin *benignus* ("kind, generous, favorable"), from *bene* ("well") + *-gnus* (related to *gignere*, "to produce, beget"). The medical sense developed in the mid-17th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The biopsy results confirmed that the growth was **benign** and required no further treatment." — *The Lancet*
2. "Despite his imposing stature, the professor had a **benign** manner that put students at ease." — *The Guardian*
3. "The island enjoys a **benign** climate, with warm temperatures and gentle rainfall year-round." — *National Geographic*
4. "What appeared to be a **benign** policy change had far-reaching unintended consequences."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** harmless, innocuous, gentle, mild, kindly, favorable, nonthreatening
**Antonyms:** malignant, harmful, hostile, threatening, dangerous, malicious, virulent

## TOEFL Tips
- Very common in **science and medical reading passages** where the distinction between benign and malignant conditions is discussed.
- Often tested as a **vocabulary-in-context** question; the TOEFL may use "benign" in a non-medical sense (meaning "harmless" or "gentle"), so do not assume it always refers to medicine.
- The contrast between "benign" and "malignant" is a frequently tested pair. Understanding both words strengthens comprehension.
- May appear in passages about environmental science where "benign" describes mild conditions or low-impact processes.
