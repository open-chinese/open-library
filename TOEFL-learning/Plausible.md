# Plausible

## Pronunciation
- IPA: /ˈplɔː.zɪ.bəl/
- Simplified: PLAW-zih-buhl

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Seeming reasonable or probable; appearing worthy of belief.
2. (Of a person) Skilled at producing persuasive arguments, especially ones intended to deceive.

## Etymology
From Latin *plausibilis* ("deserving applause, acceptable"), from *plausus*, past participle of *plaudere* ("to applaud, clap"). The meaning shifted from "worthy of applause" to "apparently reasonable" by the 17th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The detective considered several plausible explanations before settling on the most likely scenario." -- *The Guardian*
2. "While the theory is plausible, it lacks the empirical evidence needed to gain widespread acceptance." -- *Nature*
3. "The defense attorney presented a plausible alternative account of events that cast doubt on the prosecution's case." -- *The New York Times*
4. "It is entirely plausible that rising sea levels will displace millions of people by the end of the century." -- *The Economist*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** credible, believable, probable, likely, reasonable, conceivable, feasible
**Antonyms:** implausible, unlikely, incredible, unbelievable, inconceivable, improbable

## Word Family
- **Noun:** plausibility
- **Adverb:** plausibly
- **Antonym adjective:** implausible

## Collocations
- plausible explanation/theory
- plausible argument/account
- seem/sound plausible
- entirely/perfectly plausible
- plausible alternative/scenario

## TOEFL Tips
- Often appears in reading passages where multiple explanations for a phenomenon are being evaluated.
- Understanding this word is crucial for inference questions where you must identify the most "plausible" answer.
- Commonly used in academic lectures when professors evaluate competing hypotheses.
- The antonym "implausible" is equally important and frequently appears in TOEFL contexts.
