# Indispensable

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˌɪndɪˈspɛnsəbəl/
- **Simplified:** in-dih-SPEN-suh-buhl

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Absolutely necessary; essential and impossible to do without.
2. So important that one cannot function effectively without it.

**Noun**
1. A person or thing that is absolutely necessary.

## Etymology
From Medieval Latin *indispensabilis*, from *in-* ("not") + *dispensabilis* ("that may be dispensed with"), from *dispensare* ("to distribute, to manage"). The word literally means "not able to be dispensed with." Entered English in the 17th century. Related to "dispense," "dispensable," and "dispensation."

## Usage Examples
1. "Clean water is indispensable to human survival, yet billions of people worldwide lack access to safe drinking water." -- *World Health Organization*
2. "The dictionary has become an indispensable tool for students learning a second language."
3. "Her organizational skills made her indispensable to the success of the project."
4. "No individual is indispensable; the organization must be able to function regardless of personnel changes."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** essential, necessary, vital, crucial, critical, imperative, requisite, invaluable, irreplaceable
**Antonyms:** dispensable, unnecessary, nonessential, superfluous, expendable, optional, redundant

## TOEFL Tips
- Frequently appears in reading passages when authors argue that something is critically important.
- Often tested as a synonym for "essential" or "necessary" in vocabulary-in-context questions.
- The contrast between "indispensable" and "dispensable" may appear in TOEFL passages discussing what is truly necessary versus what can be eliminated.
- Useful in TOEFL writing to strengthen arguments: "Technology has become indispensable in modern education."
- Note the spelling: "indispen-s-able" (not "indispens-i-ble") -- a common spelling error to avoid.
