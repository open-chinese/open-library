# Impede

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ɪmˈpiːd/
- **Simplified:** im-PEED

## Definition
**Verb (transitive)**
1. To delay or prevent something by obstructing it; to slow the progress or movement of.
2. To hinder or block the way of someone or something.

## Etymology
From Latin *impedire* ("to shackle the feet, to entangle, to hinder"), from *in-* ("in, into") + *pes/pedis* ("foot"). The literal meaning is "to put the feet in chains" or "to catch by the feet." Entered English in the late 16th century. Related words with the *ped-* root include "pedestrian," "pedal," "expedite" (to free the feet, hence to speed up), and "impediment."

## Usage Examples
1. "Dense vegetation impeded the progress of the expedition through the tropical rainforest."
2. "Legal restrictions can impede the free flow of information across international borders." -- *The Economist*
3. "Chronic inflammation impedes the body's ability to heal wounds efficiently."
4. "Protesters blocked the highway, impeding traffic for several hours during the demonstration."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** hinder, obstruct, hamper, block, delay, retard, inhibit, thwart, encumber, stymie
**Antonyms:** facilitate, expedite, assist, advance, promote, aid, accelerate, help

## Collocations
- impede + progress / development / growth
- impede + the flow / movement / passage

- impede + efforts / investigation / recovery
- significantly / seriously / greatly impede
- factors / obstacles that impede

## TOEFL Tips
- Very similar in meaning to "hinder" -- both frequently appear in TOEFL reading passages and can substitute for each other.
- The noun "impediment" (meaning obstacle or hindrance) is equally important for TOEFL: "speech impediment," "impediment to progress."
- Interesting connection: "expedite" is the etymological opposite of "impede" (literally "to free the feet"), and both words appear on the TOEFL.
- Often appears in science passages describing factors that slow down biological or chemical processes.
- In vocabulary-in-context questions, "impede" typically replaces "slow down," "block," or "hinder."
