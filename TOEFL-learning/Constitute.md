# Constitute

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /Ààk…ín.st…™.tjuÀêt/ (British) or /Ààk…ëÀên.st…™.tuÀêt/ (American)
- **Simplified:** KON-stih-toot

## Definition
**Verb (transitive)**
1. To make up, form, or compose something. *Women constitute 52% of the population.*
2. To be equivalent to; to amount to. *This action constitutes a violation of the agreement.*
3. To formally establish or enact (an institution, law, or system).
4. To appoint to an office or function; to give legal form to.

## Etymology
From Latin *constituere* ("to set up, establish, arrange"), from *con-* ("together") + *statuere* ("to set, place"), from *status* ("standing"). Related to "constitution," "constituent," and "statute." Entered English in the 15th century.

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** compose, comprise, form, make up, represent, amount to, establish, create
**Antonyms:** destroy, dissolve, disband, dismantle, negate

## TOEFL Tips
- Appears frequently in **reading passages** about demographics, law, biology, and political science.
- The TOEFL commonly tests the "make up" or "compose" meaning. When a passage says "X constitutes Y," it means X forms or makes up Y.
- Do not confuse "constitute" with "constitution" (the document). While related, the verb has broader applications.
- Often appears in inference questions where you must determine what a set of factors "constitutes" or amounts to.
- The phrase "constitutes a violation" or "constitutes evidence" is common in academic and legal contexts on the exam.
