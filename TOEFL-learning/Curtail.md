# Curtail

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /kɜːrˈteɪl/
- **Simplified:** kur-TAYL

## Definition
**Verb (transitive)**
1. To reduce in extent or quantity; to cut short or impose a restriction on.
2. To deprive someone of something, especially rights or privileges.

## Etymology
From Middle English *curtailen*, an alteration of *curtal* ("to dock a horse's tail"), influenced by Old French *courtauld* ("short"). Ultimately derived from Latin *curtus* ("short, cut short"). The spelling was later influenced by "tail." The original meaning of literally cutting short (a tail) evolved into the figurative sense of reducing or limiting something.

## Usage Examples
1. "The government introduced new regulations to curtail carbon emissions from industrial facilities." -- *The Economist*
2. "Budget cuts forced the university to curtail several research programs and reduce faculty positions."
3. "Freedom of the press must not be curtailed, even during times of national emergency."
4. "The airline curtailed its international routes due to a sharp decline in passenger demand."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** reduce, cut, limit, restrict, diminish, decrease, shorten, truncate, abridge
**Antonyms:** extend, expand, increase, lengthen, prolong, augment

## TOEFL Tips
- Commonly appears in reading passages about government policy, economics, and civil liberties.
- Often tested as a synonym for "reduce" or "restrict" in vocabulary questions.
- Pay attention to context: "curtail" implies an intentional, often unwelcome reduction imposed by some authority or force.
- Distinguishable from simple "reduce" by its stronger connotation of cutting something back that was previously at a higher level.
