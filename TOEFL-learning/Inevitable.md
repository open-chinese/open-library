# Inevitable

## Pronunciation
- IPA: /ɪnˈev.ɪ.tə.bəl/
- Simplified: in-EV-ih-tuh-buhl

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Certain to happen; unavoidable.
2. So frequently experienced or seen that it is completely predictable.

**Noun** (informal)
3. A situation that is unavoidable.

## Etymology
From Latin *inevitabilis* ("unavoidable"), from *in-* ("not") + *evitabilis* ("avoidable"), from *evitare* ("to avoid"), from *ex-* ("out") + *vitare* ("to shun"). Entered English in the mid-15th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "Many economists argue that a recession is inevitable given current fiscal policies." -- *The Economist*
2. "The inevitable consequences of climate change are already being felt in coastal communities around the world." -- *The New York Times*
3. "Technological disruption of traditional industries is not a possibility but an inevitable reality." -- *Financial Times*
4. "As the population ages, an inevitable increase in healthcare spending will strain government budgets."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** unavoidable, inescapable, certain, assured, unpreventable, inexorable
**Antonyms:** avoidable, preventable, uncertain, unlikely, escapable

## Word Family
- **Noun:** inevitability
- **Adverb:** inevitably

## Collocations
- seem/appear inevitable
- virtually/almost inevitable
- inevitable consequence/result
- inevitable outcome
- inevitable decline/change

## TOEFL Tips
- Commonly appears in reading passages about historical events, scientific processes, and social change.
- The adverb form "inevitably" is even more frequent in academic writing and lectures.
- Often tested in vocabulary-in-context questions where you must identify it as meaning "certain to happen."
- Useful in the writing section for making strong claims about predictable outcomes.
