# Innate

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ɪˈneɪt/ or /ˈɪn.eɪt/
- **Simplified:** ih-NAYT

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Inborn; existing from birth rather than being acquired or learned. *An innate talent for music.*
2. Existing as a natural or essential characteristic of something; inherent.
3. (Philosophy) Originating in the mind rather than from experience; not derived from sensory perception.
4. (Biology) Relating to an inborn immune response that does not require prior exposure to a pathogen.

## Etymology
From Latin *innatus* ("inborn"), past participle of *innasci* ("to be born in"), from *in-* ("in, into") + *nasci* ("to be born"). Related to "natal," "native," and "nature." Entered English in the early 15th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "Research suggests that humans have an **innate** capacity for language acquisition, as proposed by Noam Chomsky's theory of universal grammar." — *Linguistic Inquiry*
2. "The **innate** immune system provides the body's first line of defense against invading pathogens." — *Nature Immunology*
3. "Philosophers have long debated whether moral reasoning is **innate** or developed through social experience." — *Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy*
4. "Her **innate** curiosity led her to pursue a career in scientific research from an early age."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** inborn, inherent, intrinsic, natural, native, congenital, instinctive, built-in
**Antonyms:** acquired, learned, developed, cultivated, extrinsic, external, environmental

## TOEFL Tips
- Extremely important in **biology passages** about the immune system, where "innate immunity" (the body's built-in defense) is contrasted with "adaptive immunity" (which develops after exposure).
- Frequently appears in **psychology and linguistics** passages, especially in the nature vs. nurture debate and discussions of Chomsky's theory of innate language ability.
- On the TOEFL, "innate" is often tested in vocabulary-in-context questions where synonyms like "inborn" or "inherent" are the correct answers.
- Understanding the contrast between "innate" and "acquired" or "learned" is essential for many TOEFL reading and listening comprehension questions.
