# Prevalent

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˈprɛv.ə.lənt/
- **Simplified:** PREV-uh-lunt

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Widespread in a particular area or at a particular time; commonly occurring or existing.
2. Dominant or predominant; having superiority in power, influence, or frequency.
3. Generally accepted or practiced; current.

## Etymology
From Latin *praevalentem* (nominative *praevalens*), present participle of *praevalere* ("to be more powerful, prevail"), from *prae-* ("before, more") + *valere* ("to be strong, be worth"). Related to "prevail," "value," and "valid." Entered English in the late 16th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "Obesity is particularly **prevalent** among populations with limited access to fresh, nutritious food." — *The Lancet*
2. "The belief that the earth was the center of the universe was **prevalent** throughout medieval Europe." — *A History of Western Philosophy*
3. "Antibiotic-resistant bacteria are becoming increasingly **prevalent** in hospital settings worldwide." — *World Health Organization*
4. "This style of architecture was **prevalent** during the early nineteenth century in the northeastern United States."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** widespread, common, pervasive, ubiquitous, predominant, rampant, frequent, extensive, general
**Antonyms:** rare, uncommon, infrequent, scarce, unusual, isolated, exceptional

## TOEFL Tips
- Appears very frequently in **reading and listening** sections, especially in passages about public health, sociology, history, and ecology.
- The noun form "prevalence" (meaning how widespread something is) is equally important. "The prevalence of diabetes" is a common phrase in health-related TOEFL passages.
- In vocabulary-in-context questions, "prevalent" is often tested with "widespread" or "common" as the correct synonym.
- Do not confuse "prevalent" (widespread) with "relevant" (pertinent to the matter at hand). They sound similar but have different meanings.
- In TOEFL reading, when you see "prevalent," it usually introduces information about how common a phenomenon is in a given population, time period, or region.
