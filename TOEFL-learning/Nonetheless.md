# Nonetheless

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˌnʌn.ðə.ˈlɛs/
- **Simplified:** nun-thuh-LES

## Definition
**Adverb (conjunctive adverb)**
1. In spite of that; nevertheless; however. Used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously.
2. All the same; even so; notwithstanding what has just been mentioned.

## Etymology
A compound of three words: *none* + *the* + *less*, meaning literally "not the less (for that)." Formed in English in the early 16th century as a single-word variant of the phrase "none the less." The separate three-word form "none the less" and the hyphenated "none-the-less" also exist but are less common in modern usage.

## Usage Examples
1. "The study had several methodological limitations; **nonetheless**, its findings have been widely cited in subsequent research." — *Nature*
2. "The initial costs of renewable energy infrastructure are high. **Nonetheless**, long-term savings make the investment worthwhile." — *The Economist*
3. "The expedition faced extreme weather conditions and equipment failures. **Nonetheless**, the team successfully reached the summit." — *National Geographic*
4. "The evidence is largely circumstantial; **nonetheless**, it points to a clear pattern of deliberate environmental degradation."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** nevertheless, however, even so, notwithstanding, still, yet, all the same, regardless, despite that, in spite of that
**Antonyms:** therefore, consequently, accordingly, hence, thus, as a result

## TOEFL Tips
- A critical **transition word** for TOEFL reading and writing. It signals contrast or concession, meaning the information that follows runs counter to what was just stated.
- In **reading passages**, "nonetheless" often introduces a counter-argument or surprising result. When you see it, expect the sentence to present information that contrasts with the preceding idea.
- In the **writing section**, using "nonetheless" is an effective way to demonstrate advanced vocabulary and the ability to present nuanced arguments.
- For **sentence insertion questions**, understanding that "nonetheless" introduces a contrast can help you determine the correct placement of a sentence in a passage.
- Interchangeable with "nevertheless" in most contexts. Both are formal and appropriate for academic writing.
