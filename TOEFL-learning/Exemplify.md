# Exemplify

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ɪɡˈzɛmplɪfaɪ/
- **Simplified:** ig-ZEM-pluh-fy

## Definition
**Verb (transitive)**
1. To serve as a typical or excellent example of something; to embody or represent.
2. To illustrate or clarify by giving an example.

## Etymology
From Medieval Latin *exemplificare* ("to illustrate by example"), from Latin *exemplum* ("example, sample, pattern") + *-ficare* (from *facere*, "to make"). Entered English in the 15th century. The root *exemplum* also gives us "example," "exemplary," and "exempt."

## Usage Examples
1. "The architect's latest building exemplifies the principles of sustainable design, incorporating solar panels and recycled materials." -- *Architectural Digest*
2. "This case exemplifies the challenges that developing nations face in balancing economic growth with environmental protection."
3. "Her career exemplifies what can be achieved through dedication and perseverance."
4. "The experiment was designed to exemplify the basic principles of thermodynamics for introductory physics students."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** illustrate, demonstrate, represent, typify, embody, epitomize, personify, symbolize
**Antonyms:** contradict, misrepresent, belie

## TOEFL Tips
- Common in reading passages where the author introduces a specific case to illustrate a broader point.
- The phrase "as exemplified by" frequently appears in TOEFL academic texts to introduce examples.
- In vocabulary questions, "exemplify" is often tested as a synonym for "illustrate" or "represent."
- Useful in TOEFL writing and speaking to introduce examples: "This trend is exemplified by the rapid growth of online education."
- The related word "exemplary" (meaning "serving as a model; outstandingly good") is also important for TOEFL.
