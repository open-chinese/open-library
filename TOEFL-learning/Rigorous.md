# Rigorous

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˈrɪɡ.ər.əs/
- **Simplified:** RIG-ur-us

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Extremely thorough, exhaustive, and accurate; following rules or standards exactly and carefully.
2. (Of a rule, system, or standard) Strictly applied or adhered to; demanding strict attention to correctness.
3. (Of conditions) Harsh, severe, or demanding. *A rigorous climate.*
4. (Of testing, training, or analysis) Intensive and exacting.

## Etymology
From Old French *rigoureus* (modern French *rigoureux*), from Medieval Latin *rigorosus*, from Latin *rigor* ("stiffness, hardness, severity"), from *rigere* ("to be stiff"). Related to "rigor" and "rigid." Entered English in the 14th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The study employed **rigorous** experimental methods, including double-blind trials and large sample sizes." — *The New England Journal of Medicine*
2. "Applicants must undergo a **rigorous** selection process that includes multiple interviews and practical assessments." — *Harvard Business Review*
3. "The university is known for its **rigorous** academic standards and demanding course requirements." — *U.S. News & World Report*
4. "**Rigorous** testing revealed that the material could withstand temperatures exceeding 1,000 degrees Celsius." — *Nature Materials*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** thorough, meticulous, strict, exacting, stringent, painstaking, scrupulous, demanding, precise
**Antonyms:** lax, lenient, careless, sloppy, superficial, permissive, negligent

## TOEFL Tips
- Very common in **science and academic** reading passages on the TOEFL, where it describes thorough research methods, strict standards, or demanding processes.
- Understanding "rigorous" is essential for questions about research methodology. When a passage describes a study as "rigorous," it means the research was conducted with great care and precision.
- The noun "rigor" appears frequently in the collocation "academic rigor" and "scientific rigor," referring to the quality of being thorough and careful.
- In the **writing section**, describing an argument or study as "rigorous" demonstrates sophisticated vocabulary and critical thinking.
- Do not confuse "rigorous" with "vigorous" (meaning energetic or forceful). Though they sound similar, their meanings differ.
