# Accumulate

## Pronunciation
- IPA: /əˈkjuː.mjʊ.leɪt/
- Simplified: uh-KYOO-myoo-layt

## Definition
**Verb**
1. (Transitive) To gather together or acquire an increasing number or quantity of something.
2. (Intransitive) To gradually grow or increase in quantity or amount; to build up.

## Etymology
From Latin *accumulatus*, past participle of *accumulare* ("to heap up"), from *ad-* ("to, toward") + *cumulare* ("to heap"), from *cumulus* ("a heap, pile"). Entered English in the late 15th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "Over the past century, greenhouse gases have accumulated in the atmosphere at an alarming rate." -- *Nature*
2. "The company has accumulated substantial debt through a series of aggressive acquisitions." -- *Financial Times*
3. "Students who accumulate knowledge gradually tend to retain information longer than those who cram before exams." -- *The Economist*
4. "Snow continued to accumulate throughout the night, leaving roads impassable by morning." -- *The New York Times*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** gather, amass, collect, build up, stockpile, accrue, hoard
**Antonyms:** disperse, dissipate, scatter, distribute, spend, deplete

## Word Family
- **Noun:** accumulation
- **Adjective:** accumulated, accumulative, cumulative (related)

## Collocations
- accumulate wealth/debt
- accumulate evidence/data
- accumulate over time
- gradually/steadily accumulate
- accumulate knowledge/experience

## TOEFL Tips
- Common in science passages about gradual processes (e.g., sediment accumulation, carbon buildup).
- Appears in social science passages about wealth, resource distribution, and economic growth.
- The noun "accumulation" is frequently used in academic writing and lectures.
- Understanding this word is important for questions about processes that occur over extended periods.
