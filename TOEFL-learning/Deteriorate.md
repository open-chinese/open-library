# Deteriorate

## Pronunciation
- IPA: /dɪˈtɪə.ri.ə.reɪt/
- Simplified: dih-TEER-ee-uh-rayt

## Definition
**Verb**
1. To become progressively worse in quality, condition, or function.
2. To wear away or break down over time.
3. (Transitive, less common) To make something worse.

## Etymology
From Late Latin *deterioratus*, past participle of *deteriorare* ("to make worse"), from Latin *deterior* ("worse, lower, inferior"), comparative form related to *de-* ("down, away from"). Entered English in the late 16th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "Relations between the two countries continued to deteriorate following the diplomatic incident." -- *The New York Times*
2. "The patient's condition deteriorated rapidly overnight, prompting doctors to consider surgical intervention." -- *The Lancet*
3. "Without proper maintenance, the historic building's facade began to deteriorate, losing much of its original grandeur." -- *The Guardian*
4. "Air quality in the region has deteriorated significantly due to increased industrial activity."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** decline, worsen, degrade, decay, degenerate, crumble, erode
**Antonyms:** improve, enhance, ameliorate, strengthen, recover, progress

## Word Family
- **Noun:** deterioration
- **Adjective:** deteriorating

## Collocations
- deteriorate rapidly/significantly
- conditions deteriorate
- health/quality deteriorates
- continue to deteriorate
- deteriorate over time

## TOEFL Tips
- Frequently appears in science and history reading passages describing decline or decay processes.
- Common in listening passages about environmental change, health, and infrastructure.
- Often paired with adverbs like "rapidly," "steadily," or "gradually" -- pay attention to these modifiers for detail questions.
- Useful in writing tasks when discussing negative trends or worsening conditions.
