# Deficit

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˈdɛfɪsɪt/
- **Simplified:** DEF-ih-sit

## Definition
**Noun**
1. The amount by which something, especially a sum of money, is too small; a shortfall or deficiency.
2. (Finance) The amount by which expenditures exceed income or revenue in a given period.
3. (Sports) The amount by which a team or competitor is behind an opponent.
4. (Medicine/Psychology) An impairment in a physical or mental function (e.g., attention deficit).

## Etymology
From Latin *deficit* ("it is lacking"), the third person singular present indicative of *deficere* ("to fail, to be wanting"), from *de-* ("away from") + *facere* ("to do, to make"). Adopted directly into English in the late 18th century. Related to "deficient" and "defect."

## Usage Examples
1. "The federal budget deficit reached a record high as government spending on pandemic relief programs surged." -- *The New York Times*
2. "Many developing nations face a significant deficit in trained healthcare workers."
3. "The trade deficit widened as imports continued to outpace exports in the third quarter."
4. "Research indicates that children with attention deficit hyperactivity disorder benefit from structured classroom environments."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** shortfall, deficiency, shortage, insufficiency, shortcoming, arrears, gap
**Antonyms:** surplus, excess, abundance, profit, overage

## TOEFL Tips
- Extremely common in economics-related reading and listening passages.
- Often paired with "surplus" as contrasting concepts -- knowing both words strengthens comprehension.
- The phrase "attention deficit" appears in psychology and education-related passages.
- In TOEFL reading, "deficit" may refer to any shortfall, not just financial -- be flexible with the meaning based on context.
- Stress falls on the first syllable: DEF-ih-sit, not deh-FIH-sit.
