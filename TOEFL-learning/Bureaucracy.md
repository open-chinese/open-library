# Bureaucracy

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /bjʊˈrɒkrəsi/
- **Simplified:** byoo-ROK-ruh-see

## Definition
**Noun**
1. A system of government or administration in which decisions are made by state officials or appointed administrators rather than by elected representatives.
2. The body of officials and administrators in such a system, especially those following fixed rules and procedures.
3. Excessive or complicated administrative procedures and red tape that slow down efficient action.

## Etymology
From French *bureaucratie*, coined in the 18th century by combining *bureau* ("desk, office") with the Greek suffix *-kratia* ("rule, government"). Literally means "rule by desk" or "rule by office." The term was first used by the French economist Jacques Claude Marie Vincent de Gournay around 1745.

## Usage Examples
1. "The federal bureaucracy has grown substantially over the past century, now employing millions of civil servants." -- *The New York Times*
2. "Entrepreneurs often cite bureaucracy as one of the major obstacles to starting a business in developing countries."
3. "Max Weber described bureaucracy as the most efficient form of organization, characterized by hierarchy, written rules, and specialization."
4. "Cutting through the bureaucracy to obtain the necessary permits took nearly six months."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** administration, officialdom, government machinery, red tape, civil service, establishment
**Antonyms:** anarchy, disorganization, informality

- Often carries a negative connotation in TOEFL passages, associated with inefficiency and excessive procedures.
