# Mitigate

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˈmɪt.ɪ.ɡeɪt/
- **Simplified:** MIT-ih-gayt

## Definition
**Verb (transitive)**
1. To make something less severe, serious, or painful; to alleviate or moderate.
2. To lessen the gravity of an offense, mistake, or punishment.
3. To reduce the harmful effects of something; to diminish the impact of a negative situation.

## Etymology
From Latin *mitigatus*, past participle of *mitigare* ("to soften, make mild, pacify"), from *mitis* ("mild, soft, gentle") + *agere* ("to do, drive"). Entered English in the 15th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "Engineers designed the sea wall to **mitigate** the effects of storm surges on coastal communities." — *Scientific American*
2. "The new drug was shown to **mitigate** the symptoms of the disease, though it did not provide a cure." — *The New England Journal of Medicine*
3. "International cooperation is essential to **mitigate** the worst impacts of climate change." — *IPCC Report*
4. "The defendant's attorney presented evidence of childhood trauma as a **mitigating** factor during sentencing." — *The New York Times*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** alleviate, reduce, lessen, diminish, moderate, temper, ease, relieve, ameliorate
**Antonyms:** aggravate, intensify, worsen, exacerbate, amplify, increase, heighten

- **mitigating** factors / circumstances
- **help** / **seek to** / **designed to** mitigate

## TOEFL Tips
- One of the highest-frequency TOEFL words, appearing in passages about **environmental science, public health, engineering, and law**.
- The phrase "mitigate the effects of" is extremely common in academic writing and on the TOEFL.
- Do not confuse "mitigate" with "militate" (to serve as a strong influence against something). This is a common error.
- The adjective "mitigating" appears in legal and ethical discussions (e.g., "mitigating circumstances"), and understanding it helps with reading comprehension.
- In the **writing section**, "mitigate" is a strong vocabulary choice when discussing solutions to problems: "Several measures can be taken to mitigate..."
