# Consolidate

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /kənˈsɒlɪdeɪt/
- **Simplified:** kuhn-SOL-ih-dayt

## Definition
**Verb (transitive and intransitive)**
1. To combine or merge multiple things into a single, more effective or coherent whole.
2. To strengthen or reinforce a position, especially one that has been newly acquired.
3. (Business/Finance) To combine the financial accounts of several subsidiaries into a single set of statements.

## Etymology
From Latin *consolidare* ("to make solid or firm"), from *con-* ("together") + *solidare* ("to make solid"), from *solidus* ("solid, firm"). Entered English in the early 16th century. The root *solidus* also gives us "solid," "soldier," and "solidarity."

## Usage Examples
1. "The company plans to consolidate its three regional offices into a single headquarters to reduce operating costs." -- *Financial Times*
2. "After winning the election, the new president moved quickly to consolidate power by appointing loyal supporters to key positions."
3. "Students should use the review session to consolidate what they have learned throughout the semester."
4. "The banking sector has consolidated significantly, with several major mergers reducing the number of independent institutions."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** merge, combine, unite, unify, strengthen, reinforce, centralize, integrate, amalgamate
**Antonyms:** separate, divide, split, weaken, disperse, decentralize, fragment
