# Preclude

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /prɪˈkluːd/
- **Simplified:** pri-KLOOD

## Definition
**Verb**
1. To prevent something from happening; to make impossible
2. To exclude or rule out in advance; to prevent someone from doing something

## Etymology
From Latin *praecludere* ("to shut off, close, hinder"), from *prae-* ("before") + *claudere* ("to shut, close"). Entered English in the late 15th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The heavy rainfall did not preclude the team from completing the outdoor experiment on schedule." — *Scientific American*
2. "Lack of funding should not preclude talented students from pursuing higher education." — *The Chronicle of Higher Education*
3. "The contract contains a clause that precludes either party from seeking arbitration for the first 90 days." — *The Wall Street Journal*
4. "Previous exposure to the virus does not necessarily preclude reinfection." — *The Lancet*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** prevent, prohibit, forestall, avert, deter, impede, obstruct, rule out
**Antonyms:** allow, permit, enable, encourage, facilitate, promote

## Word Family
- **Preclusion** (noun): the act of precluding
- **Preclusive** (adjective): tending to preclude
- **Precluded** (past tense/past participle): was prevented
- **Precluding** (present participle): in the process of preventing

## Collocations
- preclude the possibility
- preclude participation
- effectively preclude
- preclude someone from (doing something)
- does not preclude
- preclude any chance of

## TOEFL Tips
"Preclude" is a high-value word on the TOEFL, often appearing in reading passages about scientific studies, legal matters, or policy discussions. It typically appears in formal or academic contexts. Pay attention to the structure "preclude someone/something from doing something." In vocabulary questions, it is commonly tested as a synonym for "prevent" or "make impossible." Understanding the negative force of this word is essential for comprehension questions.
