# Exacerbate

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ɪɡˈzæsərbeɪt/
- **Simplified:** ig-ZAS-ur-bayt

## Definition
**Verb (transitive)**
1. To make a problem, situation, or negative condition worse or more severe.
2. To aggravate or intensify something that is already bad.

## Etymology
From Latin *exacerbatus*, past participle of *exacerbare* ("to irritate, to provoke, to make harsh"), from *ex-* ("thoroughly") + *acerbare* ("to make bitter"), from *acerbus* ("bitter, harsh"). Entered English in the 17th century. Related to "acerbic" (sharp or harsh in tone).

## Usage Examples
1. "Rising sea levels are expected to exacerbate coastal flooding in low-lying areas around the world." -- *Scientific American*
2. "The shortage of medical supplies exacerbated the humanitarian crisis in the conflict zone."
3. "Critics argue that the new tax policy will only exacerbate income inequality rather than alleviate it."
4. "Deforestation exacerbates soil erosion by removing the root systems that hold the earth in place."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** worsen, aggravate, intensify, compound, magnify, amplify, inflame, heighten
**Antonyms:** alleviate, mitigate, improve, ameliorate, relieve, ease, lessen, soothe

## Word Family

## Collocations
- exacerbate + the problem / situation / crisis
- exacerbate + tensions / conflict / divisions
- exacerbate + symptoms / conditions / effects
- exacerbate + inequality / poverty
- further / greatly / significantly exacerbate
- only serve to exacerbate

## TOEFL Tips
- A high-frequency TOEFL word that appears in reading passages about environmental science, public health, economics, and social issues.
- Often tested alongside its antonyms "alleviate," "mitigate," and "ameliorate" -- learning all four together is highly effective.
- Common in cause-and-effect passages where one factor makes another problem worse.
- Excellent word to use in TOEFL independent writing to discuss how certain factors worsen a situation.
- Be careful with pronunciation: the stress is on the second syllable (ig-ZAS-ur-bayt), and the "x" is pronounced as /gz/.
