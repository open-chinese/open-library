# Tenuous

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˈtɛn.ju.əs/
- **Simplified:** TEN-yoo-us

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Very weak or slight; insubstantial
2. Very slender or fine; having little thickness
3. Having little substance or significance; flimsy or vague

## Etymology
From Latin *tenuis* ("thin, slender, meager"), related to *tendere* ("to stretch"). Entered English in the late 16th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The connection between the two archaeological sites remains tenuous, with only circumstantial evidence linking them." — *Antiquity Journal*
2. "Peace in the region is tenuous at best, with underlying tensions threatening to resurface at any moment." — *Foreign Affairs*
3. "The researcher acknowledged that the correlation was tenuous and required further investigation." — *Journal of Experimental Psychology*
4. "The tenuous atmosphere of Mars is far too thin to support liquid water on the surface." — *Planetary Science Journal*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** weak, flimsy, fragile, thin, insubstantial, slight, dubious, shaky, questionable
**Antonyms:** strong, substantial, solid, firm, robust, convincing, sound

## Word Family
- **Tenuously** (adverb): in a tenuous manner
- **Tenuousness** (noun): the quality of being tenuous
- **Tenuity** (noun): lack of substance or strength; thinness

## Collocations
- tenuous connection
- tenuous link
- tenuous relationship
- tenuous hold
- tenuous evidence
- at best tenuous
- increasingly tenuous

## TOEFL Tips
"Tenuous" appears in TOEFL reading passages when authors describe weak arguments, fragile situations, or thin evidence. It is often used in academic discussions to cast doubt on a claim or connection. In vocabulary questions, the correct answer is usually "weak" or "insubstantial." This word is particularly important in passages about scientific debates or historical analyses where the strength of evidence is being evaluated. Understanding "tenuous" helps with inference questions, as its use signals that the author views something as unreliable or unconvincing.
