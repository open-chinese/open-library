# Undermine

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˌʌn.dərˈmaɪn/
- **Simplified:** un-dur-MINE

## Definition
**Verb (transitive)**
1. To damage, weaken, or destroy gradually or insidiously; to erode the base or foundation of something.
2. To lessen the effectiveness, power, or ability of something or someone, especially through indirect or covert means.
3. (Literal) To dig or excavate beneath a building, fortification, or other structure, causing it to collapse.

## Etymology
From Middle English *underminen*, from *under-* + *minen* ("to dig a mine, dig a tunnel under"). The literal meaning was to dig a tunnel under an enemy fortification to cause it to collapse. The figurative meaning ("to weaken gradually") developed from this military tactic. First recorded in the early 14th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "Corruption in public institutions **undermines** citizens' trust in democratic governance." — *The Economist*
2. "The spread of misinformation can **undermine** public health efforts, including vaccination campaigns." — *World Health Organization*
3. "Chronic stress **undermines** the immune system, making individuals more susceptible to illness." — *The New England Journal of Medicine*
4. "Critics warned that the new policy would **undermine** the progress made in environmental protection over the past decade." — *The New York Times*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** weaken, erode, sabotage, subvert, compromise, diminish, impair, damage, threaten, destabilize
**Antonyms:** strengthen, reinforce, bolster, support, fortify, uphold, sustain, enhance

## TOEFL Tips
- One of the most frequently tested words on the TOEFL, appearing across **all academic disciplines** in reading passages.
- "Undermine" implies a gradual, often indirect process of weakening, not a sudden or overt attack. This nuance is important for answering inference questions.
- Common in **rhetorical purpose** questions where the author discusses factors that "undermine" a theory, argument, or institution.
- In the **writing section**, "undermine" is a powerful word for discussing how evidence weakens a counterargument: "This evidence undermines the claim that..."
- Often paired with "bolster" or "reinforce" as an antonym in TOEFL passages. Understanding this contrast helps with comparative questions.
