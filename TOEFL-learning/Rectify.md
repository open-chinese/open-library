# Rectify

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˈrɛk.tɪ.faɪ/
- **Simplified:** REK-tih-fy

## Definition
**Verb**
1. To put right; to correct or fix something that is wrong or inaccurate
2. (Chemistry) To purify or refine, especially by repeated distillation
3. (Electrical Engineering) To convert alternating current to direct current

## Etymology
From Latin *rectificare* ("to make right"), from *rectus* ("right, straight") + *-ficare* (from *facere*, "to make"). Entered English in the late 14th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The company took immediate steps to rectify the errors found in the financial statements." — *The Wall Street Journal*
2. "Engineers worked around the clock to rectify the software glitch that had disrupted online services." — *Wired*
3. "The new legislation aims to rectify longstanding inequalities in the education system." — *The Chronicle of Higher Education*
4. "The organization acknowledged the oversight and pledged to rectify the situation within 30 days." — *The New York Times*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** correct, fix, remedy, amend, resolve, repair, redress, set right
**Antonyms:** worsen, damage, aggravate, exacerbate, impair, corrupt

## Word Family
- **Rectification** (noun): the act of rectifying; correction
- **Rectifier** (noun): a device that converts AC to DC; something that rectifies
- **Rectifiable** (adjective): capable of being rectified

## Collocations

## TOEFL Tips
"Rectify" appears in TOEFL reading passages about policy, management, and problem-solving. It is a formal verb that signals corrective action in response to an identified problem. In vocabulary questions, "correct" and "fix" are the most common correct synonyms. The word implies that there is something wrong that needs to be made right, not just improved. In the TOEFL writing section, "rectify" is an effective word for discussing solutions to problems, particularly in independent writing tasks that ask how to address social or educational issues. Its technical meanings in chemistry and engineering may also appear in science passages.
