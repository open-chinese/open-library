# Penchant

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˈpɛn.tʃənt/
- **Simplified:** PEN-chunt

## Definition
**Noun**
1. A strong or habitual liking for something or tendency to do something; a marked preference or inclination

## Etymology
From French *penchant* ("leaning, inclination"), present participle of *pencher* ("to incline, lean"), from Vulgar Latin *pendicare*, from Latin *pendere* ("to hang, weigh"). Entered English in the late 17th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The architect was known for her penchant for incorporating natural materials into modernist designs." — *Architectural Digest*
2. "His penchant for meticulous record-keeping proved invaluable during the audit." — *The Financial Times*
3. "The novelist's penchant for complex, multi-layered narratives earned both critical acclaim and reader devotion." — *The New York Review of Books*
4. "The region has a penchant for innovation, producing a disproportionate number of technology startups." — *MIT Technology Review*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** inclination, liking, fondness, preference, taste, predilection, propensity, proclivity, affinity
**Antonyms:** aversion, dislike, distaste, antipathy, disinclination, reluctance

## Word Family
- "Penchant" is primarily used as a standalone noun. It does not have widely used verb, adjective, or adverb forms in English, as it was borrowed directly from French.

## Collocations
- penchant for
- have a penchant
- known for a penchant
- strong penchant
- natural penchant
- particular penchant

## TOEFL Tips
"Penchant" is a sophisticated vocabulary word that appears in TOEFL reading passages, often describing a person's or group's characteristic preference or tendency. It is almost always followed by "for" (penchant for something). In vocabulary-in-context questions, "preference," "inclination," or "fondness" are typical correct answers. Because it is borrowed from French, some test-takers may not recognize it, so it is worth memorizing. Using "penchant" in the TOEFL writing section demonstrates advanced vocabulary and can strengthen your score, particularly when discussing preferences, tendencies, or cultural characteristics.
