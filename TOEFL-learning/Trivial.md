# Trivial

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˈtrɪv.i.əl/
- **Simplified:** TRIV-ee-ul

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Of little value or importance; insignificant
2. Concerned only with trifling or unimportant things
3. (Mathematics) Relating to or being the simplest or most basic case of something

## Etymology
From Latin *trivialis* ("common, ordinary, of the crossroads"), from *trivium* ("a place where three roads meet, a public place"), from *tri-* ("three") + *via* ("road"). The sense evolved from "common, found everywhere" to "of little importance." Entered English in the late 15th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "What may appear to be a trivial distinction in terminology can have profound implications for legal interpretation." — *Harvard Law Review*
2. "The researchers dismissed the anomaly as trivial, only to discover later that it was a significant finding." — *Science*
3. "No detail is too trivial when conducting a thorough forensic investigation." — *Journal of Forensic Sciences*
4. "In mathematics, the trivial solution to the equation is x = 0, but the non-trivial solutions are of greater interest." — *Linear Algebra and Its Applications*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** insignificant, unimportant, minor, negligible, trifling, petty, inconsequential, frivolous
**Antonyms:** significant, important, substantial, crucial, major, consequential, vital

## Word Family
- **Trivially** (adverb): in a trivial manner
- **Triviality** (noun): the quality of being trivial; a trivial matter
- **Trivialize** (verb): to make something seem less important or significant than it is
- **Trivia** (noun): unimportant facts or details

## Collocations
- trivial matter
- trivial detail
- trivial pursuit
- far from trivial
- seemingly trivial
- non-trivial (especially in academic contexts)

## TOEFL Tips
"Trivial" is commonly found in TOEFL reading and listening passages, often in academic discussions where the importance of something is being evaluated. It frequently appears in contrast with words like "significant" or "critical." In science passages, "non-trivial" is a common academic term meaning "significant" or "worthy of attention." When tested as a vocabulary question, the best synonym is usually "insignificant" or "unimportant." Understanding this word is also helpful for the TOEFL writing section, where you can use it to dismiss weaker counterarguments.
