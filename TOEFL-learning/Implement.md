# Implement

## Pronunciation
- **Noun:** /ˈɪm.plɪ.mənt/ — IM-plih-munt
- **Verb:** /ˈɪm.plɪ.mɛnt/ — IM-plih-ment

## Definition
**Verb (transitive)**
1. To put a plan, decision, agreement, or policy into effect; to carry out or execute.
2. To provide the means for the practical realization of something.
3. To fulfill or satisfy (a requirement or condition).

**Noun (countable)**
1. A tool, utensil, or piece of equipment used for a particular purpose, especially in farming or manual work.

## Etymology
From Medieval Latin *implementum* ("a filling up"), from Latin *implere* ("to fill up, fulfill"), from *in-* ("in") + *plere* ("to fill"). The noun sense ("tool") emerged in the 15th century from Scottish English. The verb sense ("to put into effect") developed in the early 18th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The government plans to **implement** new environmental regulations by the end of the fiscal year." — *The Washington Post*
2. "Schools across the district began to **implement** a revised curriculum focusing on critical thinking skills." — *Education Week*
3. "The software team successfully **implemented** the security updates ahead of schedule." — *MIT Technology Review*
4. "Stone **implements** found at the excavation site suggest the area was inhabited over 10,000 years ago." — *Archaeology Magazine*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms (verb):** execute, carry out, enact, enforce, apply, put into practice, realize, effectuate
**Synonyms (noun):** tool, instrument, utensil, device, apparatus, equipment
**Antonyms (verb):** neglect, ignore, abandon, disregard, revoke, repeal

## TOEFL Tips
- One of the most frequently tested verbs in TOEFL reading, especially in passages about government policy, education reform, and technology deployment.
- The noun "implementation" is equally important and often appears in passages discussing the challenges of putting plans into action.
- In the TOEFL, "implement" almost always appears as a verb meaning "to carry out." The noun meaning "tool" appears less frequently, typically in archaeology or history passages.
- In **integrated writing and speaking** tasks, you may need to describe how a plan or solution is "implemented." Knowing this word well helps you express ideas precisely.
- Pay attention to whether a passage discusses implementation challenges — this is a common topic for detail and inference questions.
