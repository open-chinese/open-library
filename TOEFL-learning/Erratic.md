# Erratic

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ɪˈrætɪk/
- **Simplified:** ih-RAT-ik

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Not even or regular in pattern or movement; unpredictable and inconsistent.
2. Deviating from the normal or expected course; irregular in behavior or performance.

**Noun (Geology)**
1. A rock or boulder that has been transported by glacial action and deposited far from its place of origin (also called an "erratic boulder").

## Etymology
From Latin *erraticus* ("wandering"), from *errare* ("to wander, to stray, to err"). Entered English in the 14th century through Old French *erratique*. The same Latin root gives us "error," "errant," and "erroneous." The geological sense developed in the 19th century, referring to rocks that "wandered" from their original locations via glaciers.

## Usage Examples
1. "The stock market displayed erratic behavior throughout the week, swinging between significant gains and sharp losses." -- *Bloomberg*
2. "His erratic driving caught the attention of a highway patrol officer, who pulled him over."
3. "Weather patterns in the region have become increasingly erratic, making agricultural planning extremely difficult."
4. "Glacial erratics found in the English countryside provide evidence of ancient ice sheets that once covered the region."

**Antonyms:** consistent, steady, predictable, regular, stable, reliable, uniform

## TOEFL Tips
- Common in reading passages about climate, ecology, psychology, and economics.
- Often used to describe weather patterns, animal behavior, or market performance.
- Tested as a synonym for "unpredictable" or "inconsistent" in vocabulary questions.
- The geological usage (glacial erratics) may appear in earth science or geology lectures in the listening section.
- Do not confuse "erratic" with "erotic" -- they have completely different meanings and origins.
