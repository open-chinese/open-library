# Advocate

## Pronunciation
- **Verb:** IPA: /ˈæd.və.keɪt/ -- Simplified: AD-vuh-kayt
- **Noun:** IPA: /ˈæd.və.kət/ -- Simplified: AD-vuh-kuht

## Definition
**Verb**
1. To publicly recommend or support a particular cause, policy, or course of action.
2. To speak or write in favor of something.

**Noun**
1. A person who publicly supports or recommends a particular cause or policy.
2. A person who pleads on someone else's behalf; an intercessor.
3. (Law, chiefly British) A lawyer, especially one who pleads cases in a higher court.

## Etymology
From Latin *advocatus* ("one called to aid"), past participle of *advocare* ("to call to one's aid"), from *ad-* ("to") + *vocare* ("to call"). Entered English in the 14th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "Consumer groups advocate stricter regulations on data privacy to protect individuals from corporate surveillance." -- *The New York Times*
2. "She has been a tireless advocate for education reform throughout her career in public service." -- *The Washington Post*
3. "Environmental advocates argue that the proposed pipeline would cause irreparable damage to local ecosystems." -- *The Guardian*
4. "The report advocates a more balanced approach to economic development, one that accounts for environmental sustainability."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** (verb) promote, champion, support, endorse, recommend; (noun) supporter, proponent, champion, backer
**Antonyms:** (verb) oppose, criticize, discourage; (noun) opponent, critic, adversary, detractor

## Word Family
- **Noun:** advocate, advocacy, advocation
- **Verb:** advocate
- **Adjective:** (none standard)

## Collocations
- advocate for/of (something)
- strong/passionate advocate
- consumer/patient/human rights advocate
- advocate change/reform
- advocacy group/organization

## TOEFL Tips
- Pay close attention to the pronunciation difference between the noun and verb forms -- this distinction is tested in listening sections.
- Commonly appears in reading passages about social movements, policy debates, and legal issues.
- "Advocacy" is the noun form most commonly used in academic contexts.
- In the writing section, "advocate" is an effective verb for presenting arguments and recommendations.
