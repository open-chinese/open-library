# Augment

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ɔːɡˈmɛnt/
- **Simplified:** awg-MENT

## Definition
**Verb (transitive)**
1. To make something greater by adding to it; to increase the size, extent, or quantity of something.
2. To supplement or enhance something that already exists.

## Etymology
From Late Latin *augmentare*, from Latin *augmentum* ("an increase"), derived from *augere* ("to increase, to grow"). Related to the word "auction" (a sale where prices increase). Entered English in the 15th century through Old French *augmenter*.

## Usage Examples
1. "The company decided to augment its workforce by hiring fifty additional engineers to meet growing demand." -- *The Wall Street Journal*
2. "New satellite data will augment the information already gathered by ground-based observatories."
3. "She took on freelance work to augment her income during the economic downturn."
4. "The library augmented its digital collection with thousands of newly digitized manuscripts."

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** increase, enhance, supplement, boost, expand, amplify, reinforce, enlarge
**Antonyms:** diminish, reduce, decrease, curtail, lessen, deplete

## TOEFL Tips
- Frequently appears in reading passages about technology, economics, and scientific research.
- In listening sections, it may appear in academic lectures discussing how researchers enhance their data or methods.
- Often tested as a synonym for "increase" or "supplement" in vocabulary-in-context questions.
