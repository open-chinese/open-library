# Feasible

## Pronunciation
- **IPA:** /ˈfiː.zə.bəl/
- **Simplified:** FEE-zuh-bul

## Definition
**Adjective**
1. Possible and practical to accomplish or carry out; capable of being done or achieved.
2. Likely or probable; reasonable to expect.
3. (Of a plan, method, or idea) Realistic and workable given the available resources and constraints.

## Etymology
From Anglo-French *faisible*, from Old French *faisable* ("doable"), from *fais-*, stem of *faire* ("to do, make"), from Latin *facere* ("to do, make"). Related to "fact," "factory," and "feasance." Entered English in the 15th century.

## Usage Examples
1. "The engineers determined that constructing a tunnel beneath the river was technically **feasible** but extremely costly." — *Scientific American*
2. "Renewable energy advocates argue that a complete transition away from fossil fuels is economically **feasible** within the next two decades." — *The Economist*
3. "The committee concluded that the proposed timeline was not **feasible** given the current budget constraints." — *Harvard Business Review*
4. "Researchers are investigating whether it is **feasible** to use gene therapy to treat this class of genetic disorders." — *Nature*

## Synonyms & Antonyms
**Synonyms:** practicable, viable, achievable, attainable, workable, realistic, possible, doable
**Antonyms:** infeasible, impracticable, unworkable, unrealistic, impossible, unattainable, unviable

## TOEFL Tips
- Common in **reading passages** about technology, engineering, urban planning, and environmental policy where the practicality of solutions is discussed.
- The noun form "feasibility" appears frequently, especially in the collocation "feasibility study," which refers to a formal assessment of whether a project can be done.
- In the TOEFL, "feasible" is often contrasted with ideal or theoretical solutions. A "feasible" approach is one that can actually be implemented, not merely imagined.
- Watch for questions that ask you to identify which of several proposed solutions a passage describes as "feasible" — this tests comprehension of the author's evaluation.
